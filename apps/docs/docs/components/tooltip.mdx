### Position

<p>
  Tooltips uses four positions in relation to the trigger element: bottom, top,
  left, and right. Make sure the direction and position of your tooltip don’t
  obscure information on the page that the tooltip content is directly related
  to.
</p>

### Pointer

<p>
  Tooltip has four pointer options: start, center, end, and no pointer. Center
  is the default pointer position. If the tooltip does not fit without being
  clipped by the side of the page, then it should dynamically pick a pointer
  orientation that fits in the space between the grid margins.
</p>

## Writing Guidance
<div>
  <h3>Always Add Value</h3>
  <p>Only use tooltips when they contribute valuable insights or supplementary information that enhances the user's understanding. Avoid the obvious or repeating what's already clear from the interface.</p>
  <div class='exampleImages'>
    <div class='wrapper -do'>
      <p class='-do'>Do</p>
      <figure>
        <img src='/img/Content_Guidance_Images/Tooltip/DO_Tooltip_Value.png' alt='Tooltip advising to include a building number for the address field.' />
        <figcaption></figcaption>
      </figure>
    </div>

    <div class='wrapper -dont'>
      <p class='-dont'>Don't</p>
      <figure>
        <img src='/img/Content_Guidance_Images/Tooltip/DONT_Tooltip_Value.png' alt='Toolip that state the obvious "Address goes here" above the address field.' />
        <figcaption></figcaption>
      </figure>
    </div>
  </div>

  <h3>Avoid Critical Information</h3>
  <p>Tooltips should be reserved for non-essential, supportive content that is not critical to a task or its completion. When key information is needed, always present it in plain sight.</p>
  <div class='exampleImages'>
    <div class='wrapper -do'>
      <p class='-do'>Do</p>
      <figure>
        <img src='/img/Content_Guidance_Images/Tooltip/DO_Tooltip_Critical.png' alt='Tooltip advising users to avoid easily guessed passwords like "123456" above a password creation field.' />

    </div>

  </div>

  <h3>Adhere to Punctuation Rules</h3>
  <p>Use sentence case for most text, capitalize proper nouns, utilize punctuation marks like commas, periods, and semicolons appropriately to separate ideas and maintain sentence structure.</p>

## Additional Guidance

<Tables
  hideHeader
  headers={['title', 'description']}
  args={[
    
    {
      title: 'Accessibility Concerns',
      description: (
        <div>
          <ul>
            <li>
              <b>All tooltips should be able to be activated and dismissed via pointer, keyboard, and touch</b>
              <p>Users should have full control of the tooltip interaction regardless of device. Do not cause the tooltip to disappear or timeout after a set interval.</p>
              <p>WCAG 2.1: <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/content-on-hover-or-focus.html" target="_blank">
                1.4.13: Content on Hover or Focus
              </a></p>
            </li>
            <li>
              <b>Avoid covering up critical content with a tooltip</b>
              <p>As with popovers, make sure that the direction and placement of your tooltip don’t obscure information on the page that the tooltip content is directly related to.</p>
              <p>WCAG 2.1: <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/content-on-hover-or-focus.html" target="_blank">
                1.4.13: Content on Hover or Focus
              </a></p>
            </li>
            <li>
              <b>Tooltips should only contain static, text-based content to avoid additional interactivity</b>
              <p>Tooltips are specifically for plain text-based content. If you require links, buttons or additional functionality in your tooltip, use a popover instead. Avoid rich text.</p>
            </li>
            <li>
              <b>Avoid placing essential information in a tooltip</b>
              <p>Tooltips should never hide critical information. Whenever possible, avoid using a tooltip altogether and place the information where it is visible at all times.</p>
            </li>
            <li>
              <b>Tooltips should always describe the triggering element</b>
              <p>Do not add a control that is specifically there to trigger a tooltip.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      ),
    },
  ]}
