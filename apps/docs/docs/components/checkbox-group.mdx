<div>
  <p>Checkboxes can be grouped together, where each group represents a cluster of related actions. A checkbox group comprises multiple instances of our standard checkbox component, inheriting its design and states.</p>
  <p>Employing a checkbox group for organizing checkboxes yields benefits over simply placing them close to each other, particularly in the following scenarios:</p>
  <ol>
  <li>Presenting a combination of checkboxes alongside individual ones.</li>
  <li>Arranging different sets of checkboxes together.</li>
  <li>Saving horizontal space while displaying a series of closely associated checkboxes.</li>
  </ol>
  <strong>Usage</strong>
  <p>Organize checkboxs with similar functions into checkbox groups, but don't cluster them together just because of their proximity.</p>
  <p>Typically, you should stick to using only default checkbox types within checkbox groups. In exceptional situations, you may include a primary checkbox in these groups, but limit it to just one primary checkbox per group, if used at all.</p>
</div>)
