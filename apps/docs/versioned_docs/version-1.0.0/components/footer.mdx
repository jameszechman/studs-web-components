<strong>Usage</strong>
<p>Footer contains your application’s secondary menu items that perform an action when clicked.</p>
<p>There are 2 variations of the footer:</p>
<ol>
<li>Compact</li>
<li>Fullsize</li>
</ol> 
<strong>Compact</strong>
<p>Compact footers have 3 components</p>
<ol>
<li>Copyright</li>
<li>Links (up to 3)</li>
<li>Contact (Email or Phone Number)</li>
</ol>
<p>This is used primarily for all products. The links are at the discretion of the designer’s IA and need to display pertinent information, like policies or other legal documentation.</p>
<img src="/img/footer_compact.png" />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Fullsize</strong>
<p>Compact footers have 5 components</p>
<ol>
<li>Copyright and Logo</li>
<li>Social Mediau Links (up to 3)</li>
<li>Labeled Field (for email capture)</li>
<li>Contact (Email or Phone Number)</li>
</ol>
<p>This is used primarily for public sites that need the marketing aspect with the form field for capturing emails, the social media links, and a contact are for email and phone number.</p>
<img src="/img/footer_fullsize.png" />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Best practices</strong>
<p>Footer help should:</p>
<ul>
<li>Be positioned at the bottom of the interface</li>
<li>Provide links to help that’s related to the experience on the screen</li>
</ul>
</div>)

  ]}
